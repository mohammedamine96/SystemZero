Apollo 11 was the first crewed mission to land on the Moon. The mission was launched on July 16, 1969, and landed on the Moon on July 20. Neil Armstrong became the first person to step onto the lunar surface, followed by Edwin 'Buzz' Aldrin. The astronauts spent about two and a half hours exploring the site and collected 47.5 pounds of lunar material. They returned to Earth on July 24, 1969, after more than eight days in space.
